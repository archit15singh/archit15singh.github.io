From an application perspective, we can view prompts as mathematical functions that take an input text and generate a specific type of output text. These functions can be categorized into different types based on their intended applications.

## Reductive Functions

### 1. Summarization

**Use Case:** Imagine you have a long article to read, but you're short on time. You can use a prompt like this:

*Example Prompt:* "Please summarize the main points of this article about climate change."

The model will then give you a shorter version with the essential info.

### 2. Distillation

**Use Case:** If you're studying a complex topic and need a simplified explanation, you can use a prompt like this:

*Example Prompt:* "Can you simplify the concept of quantum physics for me?"

The model will give you a straightforward explanation.

### 3. Extraction

**Use Case:** You have a big pile of data, and you need specific information from it. Use a prompt like this:

*Example Prompt:* "Extract the sales data for the last quarter from this report."

The model will find and extract the data you're looking for.

### 4. Characterization

**Use Case:** Let's say you want to describe a historical figure, like Albert Einstein:

*Example Prompt:* "Characterize Albert Einstein's contributions to science."

The model will provide a detailed description of his contributions.

### 5. Analyzing

**Use Case:** You have a dataset, and you want insights from it:

*Example Prompt:* "Analyze this sales data and tell me the trends."

The model will analyze the data and give you insights.

### 6. Evaluation

**Use Case:** You want to know if a book is worth reading:

*Example Prompt:* "Evaluate the quality of this book 'To Kill a Mockingbird'."

The model will give you its opinion on the book's quality.

### 7. Critiquing

**Use Case:** You're writing an essay and want feedback:

*Example Prompt:* "Please critique my essay on climate change."

The model will provide feedback and suggestions to improve your essay.

## Transformative Functions

### 8. Reformatting

**Use Case:** You have a document, and you need to change its format:

*Example Prompt:* "Reformat this document to match APA style."

The model will adjust the document's format accordingly.

### 9. Refactoring

**Use Case:** In programming, you want to improve your code:

*Example Prompt:* "Refactor this code to make it more efficient."

The model will help you optimize your code.

### 10. Language Change

**Use Case:** You want to change the way a sentence is written:

*Example Prompt:* "Transform this sentence into a more formal tone."

The model will provide a more formal version.

### 11. Restructuring

**Use Case:** You need to reorganize the sections of a report:

*Example Prompt:* "Restructure this report to have an executive summary at the beginning."

The model will rearrange the sections as requested.

### 12. Modification

**Use Case:** You want to adapt a paragraph for a different audience:

*Example Prompt:* "Modify this paragraph to make it suitable for a younger audience."

The model will adjust the content accordingly.

### 13. Clarification

**Use Case:** If you have a confusing sentence:

*Example Prompt:* "Please clarify the meaning of this sentence."

The model will provide a clearer explanation.

## Generative Functions

### 14. Drafting

**Use Case:** You need to write a blog post:

*Example Prompt:* "Draft a blog post about the benefits of recycling."

The model will generate a complete blog post on the topic.

### 15. Planning

**Use Case:** You're starting a project and need a plan:

*Example Prompt:* "Create a project plan for launching a new product."

The model will outline the steps for your project.

### 16. Brainstorming

**Use Case:** You're stuck and need fresh ideas:

*Example Prompt:* "Brainstorm innovative marketing ideas for our new product."

The model will provide a list of creative marketing ideas.

### 17. Amplification

**Use Case:** You want to make your essay more detailed:

*Example Prompt:* "Amplify the arguments in this essay about renewable energy."

The model will expand on the arguments and make the essay more comprehensive.
